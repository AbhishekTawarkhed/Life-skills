### 1. Behaviours that cause sexual harassment

- **Verbal**: Sexual jokes, comments on appearance/body, repeated requests for dates, threats, rumors about personal/sexual life, obscene language.  
- **Visual**: Displaying sexually explicit posters, cartoons, images, emails, texts, or inappropriate gestures. 
- **Physical**: Inappropriate touching (hugging, patting, stroking), blocking movement, sexual assault, leering or staring.   
- **Bullying/Harassment overlap**: Intimidation, humiliation, personal attacks, cyberbullying, or repeated aggressive behaviour in the workplace.   
- **Quid pro quo**: Offering promotions, raises, or benefits in exchange for sexual favors, or threatening penalties for refusal.   

---

### 2. What to do if you face or witness such behaviour

- **To not ignore it**: Address the behaviour firmly if safe to do so.  
- **Document incidents**: Keep records of dates, times, places, and witnesses.  
- **I will Report immediately**:  
  - To the company’s **Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)** or designated PoSH officer.  
  - If unavailable, escalate to HR or senior management. 
- **Support others**: If I witness harassment, I encourage the affected person to report and, if appropriate, file a witness statement.       
- **Seek external help**: If internal mechanisms fail, complaints can be filed with local authorities or labor commissioners under the PoSH Act.
