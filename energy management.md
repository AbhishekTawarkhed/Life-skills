### Sleep is Your Superpower -by Matt Walker
- Sleep is vital for learning, memory, and creativity.  
- Lack of sleep weakens the immune system.  
- Sleep deprivation increases risk of cancer, Alzheimer’s, and cardiovascular disease.  
- Poor sleep disrupts genetic activity and longevity.  

---

### Ideas to Sleep Better
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.  
- Limit screen time before sleep.  
- Exercise regularly but not too late in the evening.  
- Get out of bed if I don't fall asleep after some time.
---

### Brain-Changing Benefits of Exercise -by Wendy Suzuki
- Exercise boosts mood and reduces anxiety.  
- Physical activity improves focus and attention.  
- Regular exercise enhances memory and learning.  
- It protects the brain against neurodegenerative diseases.  
- Exercise stimulates growth of new brain cells.  
- Long-term exercise increases overall brain function and resilience.  

---
### Steps I can take to exercise more
- I want to start with a small duration of 20 minutes per day.
- Go to bed early so I have time in the morning.
- Make it a habit.
- Complete all tasks the previous day and correct any mistakes.
