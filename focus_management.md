## 1. What is Deep Work?
Deep Work is the ability to focus without distraction on a cognitively demanding task. Cal Newport defines it as professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit. 

## 2. How to do Deep Work properly 

- **Set routines**
- **Set time for unproductive tasks**
- **Do not use social media often**
 

---

## 3. Implementing principles in daily life
Here are practical ways to apply deep work principles:

- **Schedule deep work blocks**: Dedicate 2–4 hours daily to focused work, ideally at the same time each day.
- **Set clear goals**: Define what you want to accomplish in each deep work session.
- **Create a distraction-free environment**: Silence notifications, block distracting websites, and keep your workspace minimal.
- **Use shutdown rituals**: End your workday with a routine that signals your brain to rest.

---

## 4. Dangers of Social Media

- **Fragmented attention**: Constant notifications and scrolling erode focus.
- **Shallow engagement**: Time spent online often replaces meaningful, skill-building activities.
- **Reduced productivity**: Social media encourages multitasking, which lowers efficiency.
- **Mental health concerns**: Overuse can lead to anxiety, comparison, and reduced well-being.
