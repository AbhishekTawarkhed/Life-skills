### 1. Tiny Habits – BJ Fogg 
Speaker's example of cleaning just one tooth or doing one pushup a day was something that made me think. It shows how small actions can overcome resistance and build momentum. The brilliance is that once you start, you often do more, but even if not, one has succeeded in forming the habit .

---

### 2. Tiny Habits – Core Message  
- M (Motivation): One's desire to do the habit.  
- A (Ability): How easy it is to perform.  
- P (Prompt): The trigger or cue that reminds .  


To make habits easier, one should focus less on motivation and more on increasing ability and tieing it to something one already does. 

---
### 3: Importance of Shine 
Celebration helps the habit become a habit by creating positive emotions. When one smiles or say “Yes! I did it” after completing a habit, the brain associates the action with success. This emotional memory is more powerful than discipline, it’s what makes the habit stick.   

---

### 4. 1% Better Every Day   
James Clear’s idea that "tiny improvements compound like interest" impressed me. Improving just 1% daily doesn’t feel dramatic, but over a year it leads to massive transformation. He emphasizes focusing on systems rather than goals—because systems are what drive consistent progress.   

---

### 5. Atomic Habits – Book Summary
The book says that true habit change is identity change. Instead of saying “I want to run,” one has to say “I am a runner.” Identity-based habits are stronger because they align with your self-image.   

---

### 6: Making a habit easier to do:  
James Clear says "the Law of Least Effort"—reduce friction. Examples: putting the workout clothes where one sees them, prepare healthy snacks in advance, or automate savings. The easier the action, the more likely that one does it. 

---

### 7: Making a habit harder to do:
Increase friction for bad habits. For instance, remove junk food from your house, log out of distracting apps, or store the TV remote in another room. By making the undesired behavior inconvenient, one naturally does it less.

###  Reflection
**Q8: Habit to form :**  
- **Cue obvious:** Place the book on your pillow so you see it before bed.  
- **Attractive:** Choose books you genuinely enjoy.  
- **Easy:** Commit to just one page a night.  
- **Satisfying:** Track progress or celebrate finishing each chapter.  

---
**Q9: Habit to eliminate:**   
- **Cue invisible:** Keep phone away from me.  
- **Unattractive:** Remind yourself how bad I would feel next morning.  
- **Hard:** Use app blockers or set a passcode for social media.  
- **Unsatisfying:** Reflect on wasted time compared to reading or sleeping.
